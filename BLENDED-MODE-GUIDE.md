## How It Works

### The Formula

**Client Charge Rate = Blended Cost Rate √∑ (1 - Margin %)**

**Example:**
- Blended Cost Rate: $100/hr
- Target Margin: 60%
- Client Charge Rate: $100 √∑ (1 - 0.60) = $250/hr

**Result:**
- You charge client: $250/hr
- Your cost: $100/hr

## Tips & Best Practices

### Tip 1: Start with Cost
Always calculate your blended cost rate first. Know your costs before setting margins!

### Tip 2: Know Your Market
Research what competitors charge. Your margin should keep you competitive.

### Tip 3: Consider Client Type
- **Enterprise clients**: Can often support 60-65% margins
- **Startups**: May need 45-55% margins to win business
- **Government**: Often 15-30% due to regulations

### Tip 4: Factor in Overhead
Your "cost" from the rate card should already include:
- Salaries
- Benefits
- Overhead allocation
If not, adjust margins accordingly.

### Tip 5: Use for Proposals
This tool gives you instant, professional pricing:
1. Build team based on client needs

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What margin should I use?**
A: Start with 55-60% for consulting. Adjust based on your industry and competition.

**Q: Can I use different margins for different clients?**
A: Absolutely! That's the whole point - enter different margins and see the impact.

**Q: Should my margin include overhead?**
A: Your cost rates should already include overhead. The margin is pure profit.

**Q: How do I explain blended rate to clients?**
A: "Based on the team composition you requested, our blended hourly rate is $X. This accounts for the mix of senior and junior resources."

**Q: What if my margin seems too high?**
A: Compare to industry standards. Remember, margin covers business costs, risk, and profit.
