## Real-World Examples

### Scenario 1: Fixed Budget Project
**Situation**: Client has $3.4M annual budget for a project.  
**Use**: **Margin Mode**  
**Why**: You need to determine how many resources and at what rates to hit your margin targets within the budget constraint.

---

### Scenario 2: T&M Proposal
**Situation**: Client asks "What's your blended rate for a team of 5?"  
**Use**: **Blended Rate Mode**  
**Why**: You need to calculate a single weighted average rate across your proposed team composition.

---

### Scenario 3: Rate Card Development
**Situation**: Building pricing for different team compositions.  
**Use**: **Blended Rate Mode**  
**Why**: You can quickly test different scenarios and see the resulting blended rates.

---

### Scenario 4: Project Profitability Analysis
**Situation**: Already quoted a price, need to optimize staffing.  
**Use**: **Margin Mode**  
**Why**: You have the budget (quoted price) and need to maximize margin through smart staffing.

---

### Scenario 5: Comparing Delivery Models
**Situation**: Should we use more offshore or nearshore resources?  
**Use**: **Blended Rate Mode**  
**Why**: You can quickly model different mixes and see the cost impact.

## Can I Use Both?

**Absolutely!** They complement each other:

**Example Workflow**:
1. **Use Blended Rate Mode** to calculate your ideal blended rate
2. Multiple that by total hours to get your target revenue
3. **Switch to Margin Mode** to verify it fits your budget and margin targets
4. Adjust as needed and finalize your proposal
